Popular Books

Crying in H Mart by Michelle Zauner is a heartfelt memoir that explores identity, grief, and the powerful bond between a mother and daughter. Zauner, best known as the lead singer of Japanese Breakfast, reflects on her experiences growing up as a Korean American, the challenges of navigating cultural identity, and the profound loss of her mother to cancer. Through vivid memories and the comfort of Korean food, she illustrates how food can preserve connection, heritage, and love even in the face of sorrow. The book is both a tender tribute to her mother and a moving exploration of resilience and self-discovery.

Harry Potter and the Sorcerer`s Stone by J.K. Rowling is the first book in the famous fantasy series about a young boy who discovers he is a wizard. On his eleventh birthday, Harry learns of his magical heritage and attends Hogwarts School of Witchcraft and Wizardry, where he makes friends, learns spells, and begins to uncover the truth about his past. Alongside his friends Ron and Hermione, Harry faces challenges that lead him to a powerful secret involving the mysterious Sorcerer’s Stone and the dark wizard Voldemort. The book introduces readers to a magical world filled with adventure, friendship, and the fight between good and evil.

Intermezzo is a literary novel about grief, family, and complicated love in modern Ireland. After the death of their father, two very different brothers—Peter, a thirty-something Dublin lawyer, and Ivan, a twenty-two-year-old former chess prodigy—find their lives diverging in unexpected ways. Peter struggles with insomnia, guilt, and tangled relationships with both his ex, Sylvia, and his younger girlfriend Naomi. Meanwhile, Ivan meets Margaret, a thirty-six-year-old arts director with her own past, and what begins as an uneasy connection grows into something profound.

New Books

The Hundred Years` War on Palestine is a historical account that reframes the Israeli–Palestinian conflict as a century-long colonial struggle rather than a clash between two equal sides. Written by Palestinian-American historian Rashid Khalidi, the book blends scholarship with personal family history to trace six major turning points, beginning with the 1917 Balfour Declaration and continuing through wars, occupations, and peace negotiations. Khalidi argues that these events reflect a sustained campaign of settler colonialism, backed by powerful international allies, while also highlighting the resilience and resistance of the Palestinian people. The book offers both a deeply personal perspective and a broader historical analysis, challenging conventional narratives and emphasizing the enduring consequences of colonialism in the Middle East.

The Lioness of Boston is a richly imagined work of historical fiction that tells the life story of Isabella Stewart Gardner, a bold and unconventional art collector who became a major influence in American art and society. Born in New York but transplanted to Boston by marriage, Isabella struggles early on to navigate high society`s rigid expectations. Tragedy strikes—she loses her young son to illness, suffers a miscarriage, and finds herself constrained by societal norms that demand submission rather than vision

The Little Frog`s Guide to Self-Care is a charming and uplifting mini-book that mixes cute artwork, positive affirmations, and gentle life lessons to help readers practice kindness toward themselves. Created by the artist Maybell Eequay, it features the “little frog”—a sweet, fashion-forward frog with a mushroom hat and fabulous footwear—as your guide through over 40 hand-drawn illustrations
